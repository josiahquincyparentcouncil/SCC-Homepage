What’s happening

The proposal

Centerfolds — one of only two remaining adult entertainment venues in Boston — is applying to relocate from the Theater District to 27–29 Stuart Street, the former Jacob Wirth restaurant site. The Boston Globe reported in November that Chinatown community members were already raising concerns when this proposal first surfaced. Now the formal licensing process is underway.

The application covers two licenses: an all-alcohol license before the Boston Licensing Board, and an adult entertainment license before the Mayor’s Office of Consumer Affairs and Licensing. Proposed hours of operation are 6pm–2am.

27–29 Stuart Street is steps from the Josiah Quincy Elementary School community. More than 700 students and their families travel this corridor every day. This location is not appropriate for an adult entertainment venue operating until 2am.

Why your voice matters

Written opposition goes on the official record

The Boston Licensing Board and the Mayor’s Office of Consumer Affairs and Licensing both consider community input when reviewing license applications. Individual letters from parents and residents are logged into the official case file and reviewed by the board members making the final decision.

Our City Liaison, Katrina Lin (Neighborhood Liaison for Chinatown, Downtown, and Leather District, Mayor’s Office of Neighborhood Services), has personally connected the JQES community with Eva Jones and Conor Newman — the officials directly handling this application. She has asked that community members email them and CC her office so that opposition is tracked at every level.

The School Community Council has raised this issue through our advocacy channels. Now we need every JQES family to add their individual voice. The more emails on record, the harder this is to ignore.
